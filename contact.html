Compliance Encyclopedia

Comprehensive Compliance FAQs

A reference list of 20 detailed compliance answers regarding corporate laws, audits, and taxation protocols.

1. What is the difference between statutory audit and internal audit?

A statutory audit is legally mandated by the Companies Act 2013 to examine if financial statements present a true and fair view to public stakeholders. An internal audit is customized by management to evaluate internal control frameworks, check risk exposure systems, and detect operational wastes.

2. At what threshold is a statutory tax audit required for businesses?

Under Section 44AB of the Income Tax Act, a tax audit is required if a business's total turnover exceeds ₹1 Crore. However, this threshold increases to ₹10 Crore if cash receipts and cash payments are limited to 5% or less of total transactions.

3. What is the GSTR-2B matching rule for Input Tax Credit (ITC)?

Under Rule 36(4) of the CGST Rules, a taxpayer can only claim input tax credit (ITC) if the respective vendor uploads the purchase invoice and displays it on the buyer's auto-drafted GSTR-2B statement. No provisional ITC claims are allowed on invoices not appearing in GSTR-2B.

4. What is Section 44ADA presumptive taxation for professionals?

Section 44ADA offers a presumptive tax option to specified professionals (doctors, engineers, IT consultants, CAs) with receipts up to ₹75 Lakh (if digital cash is under 5%). Under this scheme, taxable income is computed flat at 50% of gross professional receipts, eliminating the need to maintain detailed ledger records.

5. How does a virtual CFO service support early-stage fundraising?

Our Virtual CFO service sets up dynamic financial modeling, tracks cash burn rates, determines pre/post-money valuation multiples, drafts pitch deck financial chapters, and coordinates due-diligence data rooms required by venture capital funds.

6. What are the key due dates for filing corporate tax returns?

For companies whose accounts are subject to tax audits under companies act, the due date for filing corporate income tax returns (ITR-6) is October 31 of the assessment year. If transfer pricing reports (Form 3CEB) are applicable, the filing deadline shifts to November 30.

7. What is included under bookkeeping vs. accounting scope?

Bookkeeping deals with the daily entry of sales invoices, purchase bills, payment vouchers, bank transaction listings, and ledger updates. Accounting covers ledger reconciliation reviews, depreciation adjustments, balance sheet compliance checks, and preparing monthly MIS summaries.

8. How are government fees handled in company registrations?

All official government portal fee stamp duties, ROC registration challenges, PAN/TAN generation cards, and DSC authentication tokens are billed at actual cost. We share official PDF payment receipts generated by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) to ensure complete billing transparency.

9. What compliance is needed when a company receives Foreign Direct Investment (FDI)?

When receiving FDI, the company must file a Single Master Form (SMF) on the RBI's FIRMS portal within 30 days of share allotment. This includes submitting the Foreign Inward Remittance Certificate (FIRC), KYC reports of the foreign investor, and a CA-certified Valuation Certificate.

10. What is the process for registering an NGO under Section 8 of the Companies Act?

A Section 8 NGO company is incorporated by applying for a license using SPICe+ form on MCA. You must submit a detailed description of the NGO's objectives, draft an AoA and MoA prohibiting dividend distributions, and provide proof of address, director KYCs, and name clearances.

11. How long does it take to secure trademark protection?

Once filed, you can use the TM symbol immediately within 24 hours. However, the complete examination process, public gazette advertisement, opposition windows, and final registration certificate issuance takes between 6 to 12 months.

12. What turnover threshold triggers domestic Transfer Pricing rules?

Specified Domestic Transaction (SDT) transfer pricing provisions apply if the total volume of such transactions with related parties (e.g. subsidiaries or director businesses) exceeds ₹20 Crore in a single financial year.

13. What tax benefits does the Startup India (80-IAC) scheme offer?

A recognized startup can apply for Section 80-IAC tax exemption, allowing a 100% tax holiday on corporate profits for 3 consecutive financial years out of its first 10 years of operations, provided its annual turnover does not exceed ₹100 Crore.

14. What are the key advantages of Udyam MSME registration?

MSMEs with Udyam registration enjoy priority sector bank loans at lower interest rates, collateral-free credit schemes, electricity tariff subsidies, patent registration cost rebates, and statutory protection against buyer payment delays exceeding 45 days.

15. What is a Class 3 Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)?

Class 3 DSC is the highest security level signature certificate issued under CCA guidelines. It is mandatory for filing income tax returns, uploading MCA director documents, submitting e-tenders on government portals, and registering patents.

16. What is a systems audit (DISA) and when is it required?

A DISA systems audit evaluates the security, integrity, and operational flow of corporate accounting software and databases. It is required by financial institutions, regulatory bodies, and enterprises implementing automated inventory controls to prevent cyber fraud and financial leaks.

17. When must a company issue Form 16 and Form 16A certificates?

Form 16 (Salary TDS certificate) must be issued to employees annually by June 15 following the respective financial year. Form 16A (non-salary TDS certificates for interest, rent, contractor bills) must be issued to vendors quarterly within 15 days of filing respective TDS returns.

18. What steps are involved in representing a case during tax scrutiny?

Scrutiny representation starts with analyzing the department notice to isolate queries. We then compile ledger reconciliation sheets, draft legal arguments referencing past case laws, upload verified declarations on the e-filing portal, and represent you in video hearings with assessing officers.

19. How are Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) taxed on income earned in India?

NRIs are taxed in India only on income generated or received within India (e.g. house rent, interest, dividends, capital gains from properties). Double Taxation Avoidance Agreements (DTAA) help apply lower tax rates or secure tax credits in their country of residence.

20. What is the Vivad Se Vishwas scheme for settling tax disputes?

The Vivad Se Vishwas scheme is a direct tax dispute resolution mechanism. It allows taxpayers to settle pending appeals at CIT(A), ITAT, High Courts, or Supreme Court by paying only the disputed tax amount, securing a complete waiver of interest, penalties, and prosecutions.
